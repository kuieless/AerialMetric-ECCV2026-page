Data Collection Pipeline

A hybrid construction pipeline turns real captures, reconstructed scenes, and synthetic views into reliable metric depth supervision.

The AerialMetric Dataset

To address the severe domain gap between ground-level training data and aerial perspectives, we introduce AerialMetric, built through a hybrid construction pipeline. It comprises four complementary components:

  • Overview
  • Oblique
  • Decoupled
  • Synthetic
  • Wild

AerialMetric-Oblique (47K pairs): Built from community-released raw data and 3D assets (e.g., UrbanBIS and GauU-Scene V2). We reconstruct the scenes when necessary and project the processed LiDAR point clouds or meshes into the camera views to generate scale-consistent metric depth maps.

AerialMetric-Decoupled (4.6K pairs): Captured using an RTK-equipped DJI M300 UAV. We strictly decouple core variables for rigorous evaluation: each scene comprises 16 distinct flight trajectories, systematically combining 4 pitch angles (-90°, -75°, -60°, -45°), 2 altitudes (80m, 120m), and 2 FOVs (63°, 83°). In the figure, the dense trajectories correspond to repeated flights under controlled single-variable changes, while the sparse trajectories are supplementary oblique photogrammetry flights used for mapping.

AerialMetric-Synthetic (16K pairs): Photorealistic sequences rendered via Google Earth Studio and Unreal Engine, enabling controlled decoupling of flight parameters across 32 parameter combinations, including extreme viewpoints such as ultra-low flights and acute side views.

AerialMetric-Wild (1.1K pairs, 100 scenes): Unconstrained internet drone videos annotated with pseudo-metric depth via a human-in-the-loop scale recovery framework.

Qualitative Results

Zero-shot transfer of state-of-the-art ground-domain models (e.g., ZoeDepth, DepthPro, UniDepthV2) to aerial imagery often results in severe scale ambiguity and geometric distortion. By adapting the MoGe2 foundation model on our dataset (denoted as MoGe2-Aerial), we recover more reliable metric scale in real-world aerial scenes.

Decoupled Flight Parameter Analysis & Robustness

  • All parameters
  • FOV & Altitude
  • Altitude & Pitch

Parameters robustness: Baseline models exhibit severe, non-monotonic degradation regarding camera pitch and altitude, suffering catastrophic failures at strictly nadir (-90°) and highly oblique (-45°) angles. Our fine-tuned MoGe2-Aerial demonstrates exceptional stability across all evaluated pitch angles, altitudes, and FOVs.

FOV and altitude robustness: Robustness analysis of depth estimation models (MoGe2, UniDepthV2, and MoGe2-Aerial) across different fields of view (FOVs) and altitudes. Results report the average δ1 on the AerialMetric-Decoupled dataset.

Heatmap analysis: Heatmap visualization of metric accuracy across altitudes and pitch angles.
